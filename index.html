Module 1: What is Misinformation and Disinformation?

Misinformation refers to false or inaccurate information that is spread regardless of intent to deceive.

Disinformation refers to false information that is spread deliberately to deceive.

Continue to Module 2

Module 2: Spotting Red Flags in Viral Posts

Look out for sensational headlines, extreme emotion, or clickbait.

Always check the source. Is it credible? Have you heard of it?

Be cautious of edited or out-of-context images that distort the message.

Watch out for old posts shared as new. Always check the date!

Continue to Module 3

Module 3: Use Tools to Verify Online Content

Use Google Reverse Image Search to trace where an image was first used.

Visit trusted fact-checking websites like Snopes, PolitiFact, or FactCheck.org.

Try TinEye to find older versions or edited versions of an image.

Always cross-check information using multiple sources before sharing.

Continue to Module 4

Module 4: Evaluate Post Credibility

Use this checklist to evaluate the credibility of any online post:

  • Who: Is the author known and credible?
  • What: Does the content come from a reliable source?
  • When: Is the post current and relevant?
  • Where: Are sources or evidence cited?
  • Why: Was it created to inform or mislead?
